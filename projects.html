1962 AHVD

1962 Helpline - Veterinary Assistance Call Center System

The 1962 Helpline is a call center-based support system developed for the Animal Husbandry & Veterinary Department of Meghalaya. Designed to provide real-time veterinary assistance, the helpline serves as a crucial link between farmers, domestic pet owners, veterinary doctors, and ambulance units, ensuring timely intervention and medical care for animals in need.
Built using Spring Boot, Thymeleaf, jQuery, JsSIP, SIP, Asterisk Server, and PostgreSQL, the system seamlessly integrates call management, case registration, doctor consultations, and emergency veterinary response into a single platform.

Key Features & Workflow

  1. Call Handling & Case Registration
    • Farmers or domestic pet owners call the toll-free number 1962 to seek veterinary assistance.
    • A call agent answers the call and records essential details, including:
      • Caller’s information (name, location, contact details).
      • Animal type (livestock, domestic pets, etc.).
      • Nature of the case (injury, illness, emergency, general consultation).
    • Once all necessary details are captured, a case is registered in the system.
  2. Veterinary Doctor Dashboard & Case Management
    • Veterinary doctors at the call center log in to their dashboard to view newly registered cases.
    • The doctor can take appropriate actions, such as:
      • Providing teleconsultation by calling the farmer or pet owner for further details.
      • Referring the case to the ambulance unit if on-site intervention is required.
  3. Ambulance Unit Dispatch & Field Assistance
    • When a case is referred to the ambulance unit, both the ambulance doctor and the assistant gain access to the case details.
    • The ambulance team visits the location, administers treatment, and provides necessary medical aid.
    • Once the treatment is completed, the ambulance doctor submits an Action Taken Report back to the call center.
  4. Case Review & Closure
    • The call center doctor reviews the Action Taken Report to assess the resolution of the case.
    • Based on the report, the doctor can:
      • Mark the case as resolved and close it.
      • Reopen the case for further follow-ups or additional interventions if required.

System Benefits & Impact

  • Efficient Case Management - Ensures quick response and proper tracking of every registered case.
  • Real-Time Communication - Uses SIP and Asterisk Server for seamless call routing and direct doctor-farmer interactions.
  • Emergency Veterinary Support - Ambulance referral system enables timely on-site medical intervention for severe cases.
  • Data-Driven Insights - PostgreSQL database helps maintain records of cases, allowing the department to analyze trends and improve veterinary services.

Technologies Used

  • Spring Boot
  • Thymeleaf
  • jQuery
  • JsSIP
  • SIP
  • Asterisk Server
  • PostgreSQL

Conclusion

The 1962 Helpline revolutionizes veterinary support in Meghalaya, offering timely, structured, and professional animal healthcare services. With its centralized dashboard, integrated call handling, and ambulance dispatch system, the platform enhances accessibility and ensures farmers and pet owners receive the necessary veterinary assistance without delays.

NLU ADMISSIONS

National Law University (NLU) Admission Module

The NLU Admission Module is a comprehensive web-based application designed to facilitate the admission process for students applying to National Law University. The system efficiently manages application submissions, processing, verification, and fee payments, ensuring a seamless enrollment experience.

Developed using Spring MVC, JSP, Java, jQuery, PostgreSQL, and iReport, the module is also integrated with an SMS alert system, email notifications, and BillDesk for secure online payments.

Key Features & Workflow

  1. Applicant Registration & Verification
    • Prospective students begin the admission process by providing their mobile number and email address.
    • To ensure authenticity, the system sends OTPs to both email and mobile for verification.
    • Only after successful OTP verification can applicants proceed with their applications.
  2. Application Form Submission
    • Applicants select their desired course and fill in personal details, educational qualifications, and address information.
    • Parent/guardian information is also required, with their email and mobile numbers validated through OTP verification.
    • Applicants can upload required documents for verification.
  3. Fee Payment & Enrollment Confirmation
    • Once the form is submitted, applicants proceed to pay the application fee securely via BillDesk.
    • After successful payment, the student’s application is marked as complete and is ready for review by the university’s admission staff.
  4. Admission Staff Dashboard & Application Review
    • Authorized admission staff can log in to the system’s dashboard to review and process applications.
    • Applications are reviewed one at a time, ensuring thorough verification.
    • If necessary, staff members can request clarifications, ask for document re-uploads, or accept/reject an application.
    • The system also allows synchronization with BillDesk transactions, ensuring accurate payment status updates.
  5. Roll Number Generation & Entrance Exam Management
    • At the end of the application window, the staff can generate roll numbers for all accepted applicants.
    • The system assists in allotting candidates to examination centers for the entrance test.

Benefits & Impact

The NLU Admission Module streamlines the entire admission process, reducing manual paperwork and improving efficiency in handling applications. With automated verification, seamless payment integration, and real-time communication through SMS and email, the system enhances transparency and ensures a hassle-free experience for both applicants and university staff.

NERIE - PMIS: Program Management Information System for North East Regional Institute of Education

The Program Management Information System (PMIS) of the North East Regional Institute of Education (NERIE) is a comprehensive web-based platform designed to facilitate the planning, execution, and management of educational programs across all Regional Institutes of Education (RIEs) in the Northeast region.

Developed using Spring MVC, JSP, Bootstrap, jQuery, and PostgreSQL, the system ensures streamlined coordination between administrators, faculty, program coordinators, and participants, fostering an efficient and structured approach to academic and training programs.

Key Features and User Roles:

The NERIE PMIS consists of the following user roles and functionalities:

  1. Administration Level:
    • Oversees all RIEs in the northeast region.
    • Manages administrative tasks, user roles, and system configurations.
    • Monitors the performance, progress, and effectiveness of programs across institutes.
  2. Principal:
    • Each RIE has an assigned Principal who supervises and manages all institutional operations.
    • Reviews and approves or declines program proposals submitted by program coordinators.
    • Ensures proper execution and compliance of programs with institutional guidelines.
  3. Faculty (Teaching Staff):
    • Assigns coursework and assignments to participants.
    • Evaluates submitted assignments and records participant attendance.
    • Conducts lectures and mentors participants during the program.
  4. Program Coordinator:
    • Responsible for planning and organizing programs throughout the academic year.
    • Prepares yearly schedules and program details.
    • Assigns faculty members or guest lecturers for sessions.
    • Ensures smooth execution of trainings, workshops, and academic initiatives.
  5. Participant (Students & Trainees):
    • Can log in to the system to view assigned program schedules and timetables.
    • Registers for upcoming programs and downloads/upload assignments.
    • Provides feedback and reviews on completed programs to help improve future initiatives.

Additional Features:

  • Role-Based Access Control – Ensures data security by restricting access based on user roles.
  • Automated Notifications – Alerts users about program approvals, deadlines, and assignments.
  • Document & Assignment Management – Participants can download resources and upload coursework.
  • Comprehensive Reporting – Generates reports on program effectiveness, faculty contributions, and participant engagement.
  • User-Friendly Dashboard – Provides real-time insights into program progress and participant performance.

Conclusion:

The NERIE-PMIS is a robust educational management system that enhances coordination among faculty, program coordinators, and participants, ensuring structured, effective, and transparent academic program execution across the Northeast Regional Institutes of Education.

MSFC ORS

MSFC-ORS: Online Reporting System for Meghalaya State Food Corporation

The Meghalaya State Food Corporation - Online Reporting System (MSFC-ORS) is a digital reporting platform designed to streamline the submission, assessment, and generation of monthly and quarterly reports from district offices to the state authority. Initially developed using Spring MVC, JSP, Bootstrap, jQuery, iReport, and PostgreSQL, the system was later migrated to Spring Boot and Thymeleaf for improved scalability, performance, and maintainability.

Key Features & Functionalities:

The system facilitates structured reporting and assessment, ensuring transparency and efficiency in food distribution monitoring across the state.

  1. District Reporting:
    • District offices submit monthly and quarterly reports on food distribution, stock availability, and procurement details.
    • Reports are filled online, with options to save drafts and submit final versions for review.
    • Users can view past reports, track submission statuses, and receive notifications for pending submissions.
  2. State Verification & Compilation:
    • State authorities review district reports for accuracy, completeness, and compliance with food distribution guidelines.
    • Can request modifications or additional information from districts for clarification.
    • Once verified, the state authority compiles all district reports into a consolidated state report.
  3. User Roles and Responsibilities:
    • State User:
      • Monitors and reviews all district reports (both monthly and quarterly).
      • Can detect discrepancies in reports and notify the concerned district for rectifications.
      • Has the authority to grant editing permissions to districts in case modifications are required.
      • Once all district reports are verified and approved, the state user compiles the final consolidated report.
      • Can send notifications to a specific district or all districts, reminding them to submit their reports within the deadline.
    • District User:
      • Responsible for filling and submitting reports for either the monthly or quarterly cycle.
      • Has two submission modes:
        • Draft Mode: Allows users to save progress and modify report details before final submission.
        • Final Submission: Locks the report, preventing further edits, and submits it to the state authority for review.
  4. Additional Features:
    • Automated Report Generation: Uses iReport to generate structured, printable reports.
    • Role-Based Access Control: Ensures data security and controlled access for different user roles.
    • Notification System: Integrated alerts and reminders for report submissions and corrections.
    • User-Friendly Dashboard: Allows state and district users to track report statuses, pending submissions, and notifications.

The MSFC-ORS serves as an efficient reporting solution, enhancing accountability, accuracy, and coordination between state and district food corporation offices.

181 Women Helpline – Web-Based Call Center Management System

The 181 Women Helpline is a specialized web-based application designed to manage and respond to women’s grievances, particularly cases related to domestic violence, harassment, and other critical issues. Developed using Spring MVC, jQuery, JSP, CSS, Bootstrap, jsSIP, WebSockets, and SIP, the system integrates voice call functionality via Asterisk Server, enabling a seamless call management experience similar to traditional call center applications.

System Users & Roles

The platform supports three key user roles:

  1. Call Agents – The first point of contact for distressed callers.
  2. Coordinators – Responsible for reviewing and addressing reported cases.
  3. Managers – Oversee all system operations, ensuring efficient case resolution.

Call & Case Management Workflow

  • Incoming Calls & Case Registration:
    When a woman in distress calls the toll-free 181 helpline, the system routes the call to an available agent.
    • The call agent records the details of the case, including the nature of the issue and information about the victim.
    • Once the call ends, a case is automatically registered in the system for further action.
  • Case Resolution & Escalation:
    Coordinators review registered cases and work on resolving them.
    • They can provide counseling, guidance, and support to victims.
    • If necessary, they can escalate cases to relevant authorities such as NGOs, social welfare organizations, or law enforcement.
    • If a case is too complex to be resolved at the coordinator level, it is escalated to the Manager for further intervention.
  • Manager Oversight & Monitoring:
    The Manager has access to a centralized dashboard displaying all open, pending, and closed cases.
    • They can monitor user activities, assess case progress, and ensure timely resolutions.

Additional Features & Integrations

  • Email Notifications – Integrated email system for official communication with victims and concerned agencies.
  • Real-Time Call Management – SIP and WebSocket-based call routing through Asterisk Server for uninterrupted service.
  • Data Security & Confidentiality – Ensures sensitive information is protected and accessible only to authorized personnel.

Impact & Purpose

The 181 Women Helpline serves as a crucial lifeline for women in need, offering immediate support, case tracking, and coordination with legal and social welfare services. With an efficient workflow and real-time communication features, it enhances response times and ensures that women’s grievances are handled with care and urgency.

MegPGRAMS

MegPGRAMS – Meghalaya Public Grievance Redressal and Monitoring System

MegPGRAMS is a comprehensive grievance management system developed for the state of Meghalaya, enabling citizens to lodge complaints with various government departments efficiently. The platform streamlines the grievance submission, tracking, and resolution process while ensuring transparency and accountability.

Grievance Submission Process:

Citizens can submit grievances through an intuitive online form, attaching relevant documents and selecting the appropriate department. To ensure authenticity, each submission is secured via an OTP sent to the complainant’s registered mobile number. Once validated, the grievance is formally registered, and an SMS notification with a unique reference number is sent to both the complainant and the respective grievance officer.

Grievance Redressal Workflow:

  • Complainants can track their grievance status at every stage using the provided reference number.
  • Departments can address grievances at their level or escalate them to directorates, districts, or subordinate offices for further resolution.
  • If necessary, officials can request additional clarifications or supporting documents from the complainant.
  • If a grievance is deemed irrelevant or false, the department has the option to close it.
  • Complainants can send reminders or follow up on their grievances when required.

Integration and Notifications:

  • Automated SMS alerts are sent to complainants and department officials at each stage of the grievance process.
  • The system is integrated with Sandes, a government-developed instant messaging application, for internal communication.
  • It also supports CPGRAMS (Central Public Grievance Redressal and Monitoring System) integration, allowing grievances to be seamlessly transferred between state and central platforms through APIs.

Feedback and Appeal Mechanism:

  • Upon resolution, complainants can provide feedback and rate the response. If dissatisfied, they can escalate their grievance to the appellate authority for further review until an acceptable resolution is reached.

Technology Stack:

MegPGRAMS was initially developed using Spring MVC, Java, jQuery, JSP, CSS, Bootstrap, and PostgreSQL. Later, it was upgraded to Spring Boot for improved performance, scalability, and maintainability.

This system plays a crucial role in enhancing citizen engagement, ensuring grievances are addressed effectively while promoting transparency in public service delivery.

EL-ROOTS – A Next-Generation E-Commerce Platform

EL-ROOTS is a dynamic e-commerce platform designed to provide a seamless buying and selling experience, similar to major online marketplaces like Amazon and Flipkart. Developed using a mobile-first approach, the platform was initially built as a web application using PHP Laravel, jQuery, Bootstrap, and MySQL. Later, to support real-time, centralized data management, the database was migrated to Firebase, and the mobile application was developed using React Native for cross-platform compatibility. Additionally, OS-specific functionalities were implemented natively in Android Studio (Java/Kotlin) for Android and Xcode (Swift) for iOS.

Due to organizational policies, the full workflow details are covered under a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA), but the core structure and user roles of the platform can be outlined as follows:

User Roles & System Workflow

EL-ROOTS operates on a sophisticated multi-user system, where four primary user types interact within the platform:

  1. Administrator
    • Has full control over the platform through a dedicated admin dashboard.
    • Monitors user activity, manages accounts, and ensures compliance with platform policies.
    • Oversees order flow, product listings, and service quality.
  2. Seller
    • Registers on the platform and undergoes a verification and approval process.
    • Once verified, the seller can set up a virtual store and list products.
    • Receives notifications for incoming orders and prepares them for dispatch.
  3. Delivery Agent
    • Assigned to fulfill deliveries based on geographic proximity to the seller.
    • Receives real-time alerts when an order is ready for pickup.
    • Ensures the package reaches the customer’s designated address.
  4. Customer
    • Can only join the platform through referrals or invitations, ensuring an exclusive shopping experience.
    • Upon entering a valid referral code, the customer registers, furnishes details, and sets up a delivery address.
    • Gains access to the marketplace, browses products, and places orders.

Technology Stack

  • Backend: PHP Laravel
  • Frontend: jQuery, Bootstrap
  • Database: Initially MySQL, later migrated to Firebase for real-time data sync
  • Mobile Development: React Native (Cross-Platform), Android Studio (Java/Kotlin), Xcode (Swift)

EL-ROOTS was built to streamline e-commerce operations, ensuring a seamless shopping experience while optimizing order fulfillment through efficient seller and delivery agent coordination.

JOES

JOES – Online Examination System

JOES (JavaScript & Online Examination System) is a multiple-choice-based online examination platform developed as part of my final-year BCA project. Built using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, ASP.NET, C#, and MySQL, it provides an efficient and secure way to conduct and manage online assessments.

Key Features & Functionality

  • User Authentication & Role-Based Access
    • Faculty Login: Faculty members can log in to create and manage exams.
    • Student Login: Students can only access the system once credentials are assigned by the faculty.
  • Exam Creation & Management
    • Faculty can design and configure exams, specifying:
      • Total number of questions
      • Marks per correct answer
      • Passing criteria
    • Faculty can create multiple-choice questions (MCQs), define answer choices, and mark the correct answer.
  • Secure & Dynamic Examination Process
    • Randomized Question Order: Questions are shuffled to ensure each student receives a unique sequence, preventing cheating.
    • Timed Examination:
      • An overall exam timer tracks the student's total duration.
      • A 30-second timer per question ensures quick decision-making. If no option is selected within the time limit, the question is marked as unanswered.
  • Automated Scoring & Result Computation
    • Upon exam completion, the system automatically calculates the final score based on the number of correct answers.
    • Instant score display, allowing students to view their performance immediately.

Technology Stack

  • Frontend: HTML, CSS, JavaScript
  • Backend: ASP.NET (C#)
  • Database: MySQL

JOES was designed to enhance the efficiency of digital assessments, providing a seamless experience for both faculty and students while ensuring fairness and accuracy in examinations.
